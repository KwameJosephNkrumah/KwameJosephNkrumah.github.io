Interpreting Housing Economics with Bayesian Regression

In this project, I delve into the interplay between housing prices and regional economic status in the U.S. By examining data from over 4500 homes, I demonstrate the significant effect of average area income on housing prices, while controlling for variables like house age and size. My Bayesian Linear Regression analysis not only confirms the expected relationship but also predicts house values with remarkable accuracy. This project showcases the powerful application of Bayesian methods in understanding and predicting market trends.

Optimizing Vacation Rental Pricing: A Comparative Approach Using Ridge, Lasso, and Elastic Net Regression

This project hones in on price prediction for VRBO vacation rentals using a data-driven approach. With 13 distinct features influencing rental prices, the aim was to meticulously clean and preprocess the dataset, then rigorously apply and evaluate three regression models: Ridge, Lasso, and Elastic Net. The analysis revealed Elastic Net as the most proficient method, offering an optimal blend of feature selection and regularization to predict rental costs effectively. This project highlights the power of sophisticated regression techniques in real-world price modeling and serves as a practical reference for similar analytical endeavors.

KNN VS. LOGISTIC REGRESSION: PREDICTIVE MODELING FOR DENGUE DIAGNOSIS IN CHILDREN

This project presents an analytical comparison between K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) and Logistic Regression for predicting Dengue fever in children, particularly in rural settings with limited medical diagnostics. Leveraging a dataset encompassing 14 features with a binary outcome of Dengue presence, the study methodically splits data for training and testing, employing both 80/20 partitioning and 10-fold cross-validation. Through meticulous model evaluation based on sensitivity, specificity, and accuracy, the research concludes the superiority of Logistic Regression in diagnosing Dengue, providing an essential predictive tool for healthcare professionals in resource-constrained environments.

HARMONIZING DATA: ANALYZING SPOTIFY STREAMING PATTERNS

This project investigates the influence of Spotify playlist inclusion on song streaming numbers, controlling for release year, mode, Apple Music playlist appearances, and perceived energy levels. Data from Kaggle is meticulously analyzed using a Poisson regression model to dissect the variables affecting streaming popularity. The findings reveal a statistically significant connection: songs featured on more Spotify playlists tend to garner more streams. This relationship holds even after accounting for other potential influences, suggesting that playlist inclusion is a potent factor in a song's success. The study also includes a thorough exploratory data analysis, model diagnostics to ensure robustness, and hypothesis testing to confirm the results. It paints a comprehensive picture of how digital platforms like Spotify can shape music consumption and offers actionable insights for artists and industry professionals.

SURVIVAL ANALYSIS OF CHANNING HOUSE RESIDENTS

This project offers a focused analysis of survival trends among the elderly at Channing House, examining the interplay of age and gender on lifespan. Leveraging Kaplan-Meier and Cox models, the study reveals a decline in survival rates with increased age and a lower mortality risk for females. This concise project provides essential data-driven insights into the factors influencing senior longevity, with implications for enhancing retirement community care.
